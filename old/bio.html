Bio

I am currently a first year graduate student at Purdue University, in West Lafayette, IN, where I am a member of the Network Learning and Discovery Laboratory with Jennifer Neville. I have shifted my focus from robotics to machine learning with relational networks. In addition, I am currently a recipient of the Frederick Andrews Fellowship from Purdue.

In May of 2009 I received my Masters of Science from the Unveristy of Colorado, in Boulder. During my time there I focused on mobile robotics, working on the LAGR project with Greg Grudic.

Prior to that I received my Bachelor of Science in Computer Science from New Mexico State University in December 2006, receiving the Outstanding Senior award from College of Arts and Science.

In addition to my education, I have been a coop at NASA's Johnson Space Center and White Sands Test Facility since 2005. My projects there have covered a wide range of areas; most recently I have worked on Haptic sensing with Robert Platt on Robonaut 2.
